## What I'm doing now as an AI PM

AI isn't just a new feature category — it changes what "product" means. I'm figuring out what that looks like in practice:

- **Redefining the spec.** AI features are probabilistic. A traditional PRD doesn't capture "the model is right 90% of the time" — so I'm building new frameworks for writing requirements, defining done, and measuring quality for AI-powered products.
- **Prototyping with AI tools.** I use Claude, Cursor, and the Anthropic API to build working prototypes before writing a single requirement. The prototype *is* the requirement.
- **Exploring AI-native fintech.** What does an AI-first personal finance tool look like? What happens when your wealth manager is an agent that acts, not just advises? These are the questions I'm actively exploring — some of the repos below are early experiments.

## How I think about AI in product

A few positions I hold with reasonable confidence:

> **The PM's job isn't to write prompts — it's to define what good looks like when the output is non-deterministic.**

> **Most "AI features" are just automation with a language model bolted on. The interesting work is building things that couldn't exist without AI.**

> **Prototyping with AI has collapsed the distance between an idea and a working demo. PMs who can't use this will be slower than PMs who can.**
